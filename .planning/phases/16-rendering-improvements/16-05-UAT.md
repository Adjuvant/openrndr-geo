## Remaining Issues

### 1. Antimeridian rendering still broken

**Symptoms:**
- Small jumps (179° → -179°): Renders as diagonal wedges/bands
- Pre-split data (±180°): Renders with vertical seam lines and fill artifacts

**Root causes identified (from expert analysis):**
1. Split fragments may still contain invalid edges (closure edge jumps)
2. Overlapping fragments being rendered (z-fighting appearance)
3. Pre-split data being mishandled
4. Some fragments rejected by validity filter may be legitimate

**Key insight from analysis:**
- "Unwrapping (continuous longitude) is not equivalent to splitting"
- Rendering wants: rings that do NOT contain edges traversing the seam in map space
- This means: split rings at ±180° with intersection points, NOT continuous coordinates

### 2. Suggested next steps (from expert)

1. **Use continuity approach instead of split** - For rendering, a single continuous ring in 0-360 or -180 to 540 range works better than multiple split rings

2. **Fix closure edge** - The segment from last vertex back to first must also be checked for crossing

3. **Different handling for pre-split vs crossing:**
   - Pre-split (data at ±180°): Don't transform, leave as-is
   - True crossing (small jump): Use interpolation-based split

4. **Validate output** - After any transformation, verify:
   - Ring is closed (first == last)
   - Minimum 4 points
   - No edge has |Δlon| > 180° (including closing edge)
